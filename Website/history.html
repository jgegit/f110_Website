History

Founded in 2015 at the University of Pennsylvania's School of Engineering and Applied Science, F1/10 has quickly grown from a small club into a nationwide phenomenon. Originally concieved of by Sertac Karaman as MIT's independent activities period RACECAR course, the principles of the course were adopted by Penn Engineering students who transformed the program into a full-fledged racing competition.

PICTURE OF HEAD TO HEAD RACING HERE

F1/10 has since been adopted by a number of universities, including University of Texas: Austin and Vanderbilt University. At the conclusion of the spring 2016 semester, all teams will gather to partake in a world's first F1/10 fully autonomous racing competition. With only algorithms differentiating the teams, it is sure to be a race for the ages.
